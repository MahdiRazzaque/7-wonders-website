The Colosseum is one of the most iconic structures in the world. Located in Rome, Italy, it is a symbol of the Roman Empire and its grandeur. Built in the first century AD, the Colosseum is a testament to the engineering and architectural prowess of the ancient Romans.

General information

The Colosseum is an elliptical amphitheater that was built in the first century AD. It is the largest amphitheater ever built and is considered one of the greatest works of Roman architecture and engineering. It is made of concrete and stone and is 189 meters long, 156 meters wide, and 48 meters high. It could hold up to 50,000 spectators and was used for gladiatorial contests, public spectacles, and other events.

History

The Colosseum was commissioned by Emperor Vespasian in 72 AD and was completed in 80 AD. It was used for gladiatorial contests, public spectacles, and other events. It was also used for executions and animal hunts. It was damaged by earthquakes and stone-robbers over the centuries, but it has been restored and is now a major tourist attraction.
